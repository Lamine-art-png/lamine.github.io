Product launch · Field Intelligence

From field evidence to enterprise action.

AGRO-AI Field Intelligence turns voice, images and location into structured operational evidence. It determines what needs attention. It prepares the next action. It keeps the result inside the enterprise system that manages the operation.

AGRO-AI•San Francisco, California•July 21, 2026•8 minute read

The performance figure shown in the launch artwork is illustrative. Operational outcomes vary by crop, baseline, execution and local conditions. AGRO-AI does not guarantee a specific yield increase.

The field already knows more than most enterprise systems do. The information exists in what an operator sees, what a scout notices, what a manager hears and what a photograph captures. The failure happens next.

A critical observation enters a phone call. A voice note lands in a private thread. Images remain on one device. Location is omitted. The operational meaning is reduced to a few lines in a spreadsheet or lost entirely. Management receives an incomplete version after the useful decision window has started to close.

This is not a minor documentation problem. It is an operating-control problem. Agricultural enterprises cannot coordinate work, evaluate risk or verify outcomes when the evidence behind a decision is separated from the system responsible for execution.

The problem is not a lack of field knowledge. It is the loss of that knowledge between observation and action.

Field Intelligence closes that gap.

AGRO-AI Field Intelligence is the field operating layer of the AGRO-AI Enterprise Portal. Operators can capture conditions through natural speech, typed notes, photographs and location. AGRO-AI converts that evidence into a structured operational record.

The system identifies what is visible. It separates facts from hypotheses. It records uncertainty. It determines what should happen next. Where action is justified, it prepares work with ownership, priority and verification requirements.

Voice becomes an operational input.

Field work does not begin at a desk. Long forms slow the operator down and often strip away the context that matters. Field Intelligence starts with the way people already communicate.

An operator can record a natural observation while continuing an inspection. Live captions provide immediate feedback. The original recording remains part of the evidence. A server-side transcription is processed through the durable Field Intelligence pipeline, so the saved record does not depend on a temporary browser transcript.

The result is not an isolated audio file. It becomes searchable evidence connected to the correct operation, field, block, crop, author, time and location.

Images are evidence, not decoration.

Photographs are analyzed together with the operator’s account. The visual system can identify relevant visible conditions and compare them with the spoken context. It also states what the image cannot establish.

In the avocado workflow shown in the launch film, AGRO-AI can recognize a heavy localized crop load, uneven fruit-size distribution and branches carrying concentrated weight. It does not pretend that a photograph proves internal fruit quality or harvest maturity. It recommends representative maturity sampling and branch inspection before approval.

A serious decision system does not manufacture certainty.

Field Intelligence returns confidence, uncertainty and required verification. Important decisions remain grounded in evidence and accountable human review.

Analysis is only useful when it changes the operation.

Many tools stop at transcription or image description. Field Intelligence is built around the next operational decision.

AGRO-AI can prepare a scouting task, a sampling task, an inspection or a monitoring requirement. The task can include an owner, priority, deadline, instructions and the evidence required before closure. The original observation remains attached to the action, so teams understand why the work exists.

This creates a continuous chain from field signal to management visibility. The enterprise can see what was observed, what was decided, what was assigned and what evidence came back.

Built into the enterprise system.

Field Intelligence is not a separate mobile notebook. Every accepted observation becomes part of the AGRO-AI Enterprise Portal.

Records appear in the operational timeline and on the map. They can inform tasks, evidence review, reports and subsequent AGRO-AI analysis. With authorized data sources connected, the same observation can be evaluated alongside the other information available to the operation.

An enterprise does not need one more place to store notes. It needs a shared operating memory. Field evidence must remain available after the conversation ends and after the person who captured it moves to the next block.

Designed for actual field conditions.

Field Intelligence supports a durable offline queue. Operators can capture notes and evidence where connectivity is unreliable. Pending work remains visible. Synchronization resumes when a connection is available.

Media is stored through tenant-scoped object storage. Processing runs through durable jobs with bounded retries. Location access, media permissions, workspace authorization and commercial limits are enforced by the server. Browser controls improve the experience, but they do not define the security boundary.

Enterprise control remains the standard.

Evidence before assertion

Visible facts, operational hypotheses and unknowns are presented separately.

Human review where it matters

AGRO-AI does not convert uncertain imagery into an unsupported agronomic diagnosis.

Action with traceability

Tasks remain connected to the observation, source media, author, location and processing history.

No hidden physical actuation

Field Intelligence does not autonomously apply chemicals or operate equipment. Execution remains governed by explicit controls.
