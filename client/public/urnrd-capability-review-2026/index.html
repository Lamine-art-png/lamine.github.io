Selected work 01

Upper Republican NRD concept environment

AGRO-AI created a URNRD-specific concept using synthetic/public-context records to make the workflow tangible. More important than the interface itself was the scope correction: after Jasper explained that producers make irrigation decisions while URNRD focuses on metering, allocation compliance, annual administrative review, and long-term reporting, the proposition was narrowed accordingly.

Administrative review

Structured records for missing or stale readings, staff corrections, identifier mismatches, allocation-risk cases, and review status.

Allocation context

Annual and five-year status, district-defined groupings, long-term use, and reviewable source records.

Grounded analysis

Natural-language review of the same structured records, including summaries, reconciliation, and report drafting.

Board outputs

Formal summary concepts, appendices, quality notes, exceptions, and traceable source references.

Important boundary: The relevant URNRD fit is a compliance, data-alignment, administrative review, and reporting layer. It is not field-level irrigation approval, remote pivot control, or replacement of Producer Connect.
Selected work 02

Fox Canyon data-reliability and workflow validation

Problem addressed

Records arriving through multiple AMI providers, spreadsheets, identifiers, and delivery paths can create missing-data work and weak traceability.

AGRO-AI role

Provider-neutral intake, normalization, reconciliation, exception workflow, provenance, and review-ready outputs.

Validation structure
