Internship Program

Structured ownership, flexible execution.

The AGRO-AI internship program is remote, part-time, and project-based. Each intern receives a clear brief, regular product context, and a final deliverable that can be used in customer, investor, or technical conversations.

  • Recommended commitment: 8-15 hours per week.
  • Typical project length: 6-10 weeks.
  • Academic credit, CPT-aligned internships, and university project structures are welcome.
  • Strong communication and consistent weekly progress matter more than location.

Open Roles

Three internship tracks for high-agency contributors.

Product Intern

Shape workflows for irrigation intelligence.

Partner on product research, workflow mapping, UX copy, acceptance criteria, and demo readiness for AGRO-AI's decision surfaces.

  • Translate farmer, operator, and enterprise stakeholder needs into clear product notes.
  • Audit product flows for clarity, trust, and decision readiness.
  • Help write briefs for dashboard, report, and verification features.
Remote Part-time Project-based

Go-to-Market Intern

Turn technical value into enterprise-ready messaging.

Support market research, customer segmentation, sales materials, partner briefs, and campaign assets for agriculture and water management buyers.

  • Research growers, asset managers, OEMs, and irrigation ecosystem partners.
  • Draft concise positioning for pilots, demos, and executive conversations.
  • Organize outreach lists and help refine qualification signals.

Research Intern

Deepen the evidence behind water decisions.

Explore irrigation benchmarks, crop-water context, controller systems, verification methods, and research-backed assumptions that strengthen AGRO-AI's decision layer.

  • Synthesize public research into usable product and customer notes.
  • Compare irrigation, evapotranspiration, soil moisture, and verification approaches.
  • Help document assumptions, limitations, and evidence standards.
Remote Part-time Project-based

Who We Are Looking For

Curious, careful builders who like useful work.

  • Strong written communication and structured thinking.
  • Interest in agriculture, climate, water, AI, SaaS, or operations.
  • Comfort working independently with a clear weekly rhythm.
  • Bias toward practical outputs: briefs, prototypes, research memos, customer notes, and product specs.
  • Respect for messy real-world data and the trust required in operational software.
  • Willingness to learn fast and ask direct questions when context is missing.
